<div class="alert alert-success" role="alert">
<h4 class="alert-heading">Solution</h4>

**Problem 19**

Substitute the two points $(1,6)$ and $(3,24)$ on the graph into the function expression to obtain:

$$
\begin{cases}
Cb^1 &= 6 \\
Cb^3 &= 24
\end{cases}
$$

By dividing the two equations, we get $b^2 = 4 \implies b=\pm 2$, and then we find $C = \pm 3$. Since $b$ and $C$ have the same sign, the function expression is $f(x) = 3 \cdot 2^x$.

----

**Problem 20**

Substitute the two points $(-1,3)$ and $(1,\frac{4}{3})$ on the graph into the function expression to obtain:

$$
\begin{cases}
Cb^{-1} &= 3 \\
Cb^1 &= \frac{4}{3}
\end{cases}
$$

Multiplying the two equations gives: $C^2 = 4 \implies C = \pm 2$, and then we find $b = \frac{2}{3}$. Since b and C have the same sign, the function expression is $f(x) = 2 \cdot \left(\frac{2}{3}\right)^x$.
